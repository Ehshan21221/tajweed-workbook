📖 Lesson 3: Harakāt — The Short Vowels
LESSON 3 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Name all three Harakāt · Know their shapes, sounds, and Arabic names · Read any letter with all three vowels correctly.

📌 Harakāt (حَرَكَات) are the short vowel symbols written above or below Arabic letters. Every consonant must carry either a Harakah or a Sukūn (lesson 11).
◌َ
Fathah (فَتْحَة)
Sound: "a" — mouth opens
بَ · تَ · جَ
Written ABOVE the letter
◌ِ
Kasrah (كَسْرَة)
Sound: "i" — lower jaw drops
بِ · تِ · جِ
Written BELOW the letter
◌ُ
Dammah (ضَمَّة)
Sound: "u" — lips round
بُ · تُ · جُ
Written ABOVE the letter
⚠️ Do NOT lengthen short vowels. Fathah = short "a". Kasrah = short "i". Dammah = short "u". Lengthening only happens with Maddah letters (Lesson 7).

📌 Tanwīn (تَنْوِين) adds the sound of Nūn (ن) after the vowel at the END of a word. It only appears at word endings and is written as a double mark.

◌ٌ
Tanwīn Damm
Sound: "-un"

🌙 Noor Ul-Ilm AcademyLesson 11 — Sukūn (Jazm)
📖 Lesson 11: Sukūn (Jazm) — The Resting Sign (ْ)
LESSON 11 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Define Sukūn · Recognise its symbol · Know it means NO vowel · Read Sākin letters correctly.

📌 Sukūn (سُكُون) = "stillness/rest." The small circle (ْ) above a letter means NO vowel. A letter with Sukūn is called Sākin (سَاكِن).
◌ْ
Sukūn / Jazm
Small circle above a letter
بْ · تْ · كْ
No vowel — letter is still
📌 Key Rules for Sākin Letters:

1. A Sākin letter is always in the MIDDLE of a word — never the first or last in normal recitation.

2. Do NOT add any vowel sound to a Sākin letter — just close at that letter quickly.

3. Special Sākin letters (Nūn & Meem) have extra rules → see Lessons 12, 14, and 15.

4. At Waqf (pause), the last letter also becomes Sākin — see Lesson 21.

📖 Lesson 12: Sākin & Tanwīn — Izhār & Ikhfā'
LESSON 12 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Define Izhār and Ikhfā' · Know their letters · Apply the rules when Nūn Sākin or Tanwīn is followed by each group.

📌 When Nūn has Sukūn (نْ) or Tanwīn (ً ٍ ٌ) appears, one of 4 rules applies. Lesson 12 covers: Izhār & Ikhfā'. Lesson 14 covers: Idghām & Iqlāb.
📍 Rule 1: Izhār (إِظْهَار) — Clear Pronunciation
Letters (6 — Throat): ء · ه · ع · ح · غ · خ — Nūn is pronounced CLEARLY, no nasal sound.
ExampleNūn + LetterHow to ReadSource
مَنْ ءَامَنَنْ + ءMan — 'aamana (clear Nūn)Al-Baqarah
مِنْ هَادٍنْ + هMin — haadin (clear Nūn)Al-Ra'd

📍 Rule 2: Ikhfā' (إِخْفَاء) — Concealment
Letters (15): ت · ث · ج · د · ذ · ز · س · ش · ص · ض · ط · ظ · ف · ق · ك — Nūn is hidden with a nasal Ghunnah of 2 counts.

📖 Lesson 13: Tashdīd — The Double Consonant (ّ)
LESSON 13 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Define Tashdīd · Know its symbol · Understand it represents a doubled letter · Distinguish Ghunnah Tashdīd (ن · م) from regular Tashdīd.

📌 Tashdīd (تَشْدِيد) = "strengthen/stress." The symbol (ّ) indicates the letter is doubled: first Sākin, then same letter with vowel.
⚠️ Ghunnah Mushaddadah: When نّ or مّ appear with Tashdīd, hold nasal Ghunnah for 2 counts before releasing. This is OBLIGATORY everywhere in the Quran.

📖 Lesson 14: Sākin & Tanwīn — Idghām & Iqlāb
LESSON 14 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Define Idghām and Iqlāb · Know their letters · Distinguish Idghām WITH Ghunnah from WITHOUT Ghunnah.

📍 Rule 3: Idghām (إِدْغَام) — Merging
Letters (6) — Mnemonic: يَرْمَلُوْن
WITH Ghunnah (4): ي · ن · م · و — merge AND hold nasal 2 counts
WITHOUT Ghunnah (2): ل · ر — merge completely, NO nasal

📍 Rule 4: Iqlāb (إِقْلَاب) — Conversion
Only 1 letter: ب — Nūn converts to Meem sound with Ghunnah 2 counts.
نْ + ب → م (hold nasal Ghunnah 2 counts, close lips softly)

📖 Lesson 21: Waqf — Rules of Pausing & Stopping (أَحْكَامُ الْوَقْفِ)
LESSON 21 OF 21

🎯 Objective

Define Waqf · Know all Waqf symbols · Apply correct stopping technique · Know what happens to Tanwīn, Tashdīd, and Maddah at Waqf.

📌 Waqf (وَقْف) = "to stop/pause." Stop the voice at the end of a word with a brief breath, keeping the mouth ready to resume.
📍 Waqf Symbols in the Quran
SymbolNameMeaningRule
مWaqf LāzimMUST stop hereStopping is obligatory — continuing damages meaning.
طWaqf MuṭlaqAbsolute StopStopping is preferred here.
جWaqf Jā'izPermitted StopMay stop or continue — both are fine.
زWaqf MujawwazContinuing BetterContinuing is preferred; stopping permitted.
صWaqf MurakhkhasPermitted if neededOnly stop here if breath is needed.
لاWaqf Māni'Do NOT stopStopping is forbidden — damages the meaning.
قلىAl-Waqf AwlāStopping betterBetter to stop but continuing is permissible.
صلىAl-Wasl AwlāContinuing betterBetter to continue without stopping.
∴ ∴Waqf Mu'ānaqaStop at ONE onlyTwo marked points: stop at one, NOT both.
📍 What Happens at Waqf?
SituationWhat Changes?ExampleWaqf Reading

Tanwīn Kasr/DammDrop Nūn — letter becomes Sākinعَظِيمٌ'Aẓeem
